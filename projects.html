VoiceUp



VoiceUp was founded in 2013 as a student-led event for high school students in Hong Kong to engage in intellectual discussions about current events and pressing issues through the means of storytelling. As the founder and project manager, I have strived to make VoiceUp an event where adolescents of all backgrounds could practice public speaking and use storytelling as a means of inspiring, empowering, and supporting the youths around them. Moving to California, VoiceUp is in the process of transforming into an even dedicated to intellectual discussions and conversations related to psychology through storytelling. In this iteration of VoiceUp, we seek to educate university students at UCLA about psychology and break down the myths the general public have towards familiar topics in psychology, ranging from mental disorders to happiness, learning to pressure, performance to intelligence.

As part of my ongoing efforts with VoiceUp, I have been awarded the Bowdoin College Book Award for innovative thinking and creativity (2015).

StApp



StApp is an Android App designed to track users' social media usage as part of a study I am co-authoring with professors at the Chinese University of Hong Kong. The study aims to investigate how self-regulation interventions can affect social media usage, using previous models of inhibition and emotion regulation models. We are currently in the process of piloting the study, in order to debug the app and ensure that it performs consistently across different mobile devices.

This project was made on:  Android Studio (Gradle, Java, Javascript, XML)  |  GitHub Code

SUS



SUS, short for "SOS Under Sustainability", is a project led by me and 3 other programmers during the annual LA Hacks Hackathon of 2017. Over the span of 3 days, we were able to design an Android App that used the API provided by the City of LA to create a social networking app that promoted sustainable lifestyles through civil engagement and real-time alert systems. Users could pinpoint a location in LA that is of concern to the environment, and can then host an event that the public can attend. Events can range from beach clean-ups to demonstrations. The app was awarded the Most Sustainable Hack for its innovative means of promoting sustainable lifestyles and change in the city of LA.

This project was made on:  Android Studio (Gradle, Java, Javascript, XML);  City of LA API;  SQL (Database);  PHP (Server)  |   GitHub Code (Snippets)

BrainBound



BrainBound is a self-initiated project of mine, to provide an online social network for young and professional researchers alike. Similar to how programmers and entrepreneurs can connect and collaborate with each other through apps such as AngelList, aspiring researchers should take advantage of technology to collaborate with other researchers who share common interests, and view research as a form of entrepreneurship- an investment aimed to create and innovate the world of academia with fruitful knowledge. By designing an app that not only connects users to desired research labs, but also equips users with necessary research skills through online training courses, I hope to bring the "startup culture" to research, to bridge the skill gap between experienced professors and aspiring researchers across a multitude of fields.
