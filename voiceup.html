VOICEUP

VoiceUp remains one of my longstanding initiatives to empower young adults and youths through the art of storytelling and creative expression. Today, the student-led organization is fortunate to have expanded across Hong Kong and Los Angeles, and is now in the process of developing further into central parts of China and California. Learn more here, or on our main website!

BACKGROUND

I founded VoiceUp back in 2013 with an aspiration to revolutionize the opportunities youths and students had to share their experiences and inspire their peers. Though TED Talks offerred a similar opportunity, I felt that did not offer equal and ample opportunities for students in general to share their stories. Given the limitations of TED in achieving what I believe is a powerful source of inspiration for adolescents worldwide, I founded VoiceUp.



Since 2013, VoiceUp has worked with numerous numbers of organizations and schools across Hong Kong, and we are now in the process of expanding into Beijing and California. I am currently in charge of opening a new chapter of VoiceUp at UCLA, to inspire college students by debunking common misconceptions students have about human psychology and mental health. As a result of my efforts, I was awarded the Bowdoin Book Prize for Creative Innovation in 2015.

WHAT WE DO

One of VoiceUp's core mission is to start a conversation between the inspirations that share their stories on stage, and the audience of students that sit and listen. In our events, we offer workshops before and after stories, led by the speakers/performers themselves, which allows the audience to further engage and immerse themselves in the inspirational experience. In addition, we offer a discussion panel towards the end of our events, to ensure that the audience have their voices heard.



In addition to our events, I also started a video series under VoiceUp, in hopes of driving forward the stories and conversations youths can have about ongoing issues relevant to their personal development and livelihood. I see the great engagement of younger generations on social media and am willing to use this platform to capitalize on the inspiration that we can offer for young adults alike.
